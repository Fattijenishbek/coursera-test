How did he succeed in keeping this interrelationship between speaker, speech, and audience alive from one speaking engagement to the next? "I visit a town or city," he wrote, "and try to arrive there early enough to see the postnaster, the barber, the hotel manager, the principal of the school, some of the ministe, and then go into the stores and talk with people, and see what has been their gistory and opportunities they had.
